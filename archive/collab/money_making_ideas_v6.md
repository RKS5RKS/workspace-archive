# 15 Creative Money‑Making Ideas (Budget < $200)

Below are fifteen low‑cost side‑hustles aimed at **under‑$200 startup cost**. Each idea targets a specific pain point that is largely unserved in the listed market segments.

---

## 1️⃣ Small‑Business Services (restaurants, shops, salons)

**Idea 1 – “Menu‑Print Refresh Kit”**
- **Problem:** Independent eateries constantly need fresh, eye‑catching menu prints but lack design time and cheap printing solutions. 
- **Solution:** Offer a $50 starter kit: a set of high‑resolution, editable Canva templates + a partnership with a local printer (discounted bulk prints). You handle the design tweaks and ordering for each menu update.

**Idea 2 – “Window‑Display Rental Pack”**
- **Problem:** Small retail shops want rotating window displays for seasonal sales but can’t afford custom signage.
- **Solution:** Assemble a reusable “display kit” (magnetic boards, interchangeable graphics) for $150. Charge a weekly rental/maintenance fee and update graphics for holidays.

**Idea 3 – “Salon Appointment‑No‑Show Insurance”**
- **Problem:** Salons lose revenue when clients miss appointments.
- **Solution:** Sell a $5 per‑client “no‑show guarantee” where you collect a small pre‑payment and, if the client is late/no‑show, you keep the fee for the salon. Minimal admin with a simple Google Form and Stripe.

---

## 2️⃣ College‑Student Services

**Idea 4 – “Dorm‑Room Mini‑Garden Starter”**
- **Problem:** Students want fresh herbs for cooking but have no space or knowledge.
- **Solution:** Create a $30 kit (compact hydroponic tray, seeds, LED light). Offer a weekly text‑check‑in service for plant care tips (subscription via PayPal).

**Idea 5 – “Exam‑Prep Flash‑Card App (No‑Code)​**
- **Problem:** Students need affordable, custom flashcards for each course.
- **Solution:** Use a no‑code platform (e.g., Glide) to build a simple flash‑card web app; charge a $3‑$5 per‑semester access fee. Initial cost: domain and platform subscription < $100.

**Idea 6 – “Campus‑Bike‑Lock Rental”**
- **Problem:** Many campuses have bike‑theft issues; students need reliable locks but can’t justify buying expensive ones.
- **Solution:** Purchase a set of high‑quality U‑locks (≈ $150 total) and rent them out on a per‑day basis via a QR‑code checkout system (Google Sheet + QR codes). Low overhead, high turnover.

---

## 3️⃣ Senior Services

**Idea 7 – “Tech‑Buddy Home Visits (30‑min)**
- **Problem:** Seniors often struggle with setting up new devices (smartphones, tablets) and feel isolated.
- **Solution:** Offer a $30‑per‑visit “quick‑setup” service (install apps, teach video‑calls). Use a simple booking page (Calendly free tier) and travel within a small radius to keep costs low.

**Idea 8 – “Medication‑Reminder Voice Cards**
- **Problem:** Forgetting meds is a common health risk for seniors.
- **Solution:** Record personalized voice reminders on inexpensive programmable MP3 players ($15 each) and sell a set for $70. Provide a simple setup guide.

---

## 4️⃣ Gig‑Economy Help

**Idea 9 – “Gig‑Pro Portfolio Builder”**
- **Problem:** New gig workers (drivers, freelancers) lack professional-looking portfolios to attract higher‑pay clients.
- **Solution:** Offer a $100 service to create a one‑page PDF portfolio with branding, rates, and testimonials using Canva. Deliver digitally; no physical costs.

**Idea 10 – “Shift‑Swap Alert Bot (Telegram)"**
- **Problem:** Gig workers often need last‑minute shift swaps but rely on messy group chats.
- **Solution:** Set up a Telegram bot (hosted on a free Heroku‑type tier) that posts open shift alerts. Charge a $5/month subscription for premium alerts. Initial dev cost < $200 (if you outsource a simple bot).

---

## 5️⃣ Emergency‑Preparedness Niche

**Idea 11 – “15‑Minute Shelter Kit Assembly Service"**
- **Problem:** Many families have basic supplies but lack a ready‑to‑go emergency bag.
- **Solution:** Offer a hands‑on assembly service for $150: you purchase a bulk “starter kit” (first‑aid, water pouches, thermal blanket) and help families pack/customize their own bag on the spot.

**Idea 12 – “Pet‑Safe Emergency Plan Pack"**
- **Problem:** Pet owners often forget to include pets in disaster plans.
- **Solution:** Create a $50 printable guide plus a small “pet‑kit” (collar tag, portable water bottle). Sell bundles and offer a one‑hour consultation on pet‑friendly evacuation routes.

---

## 6️⃣ AI Art/Design Services

**Idea 13 – “AI‑Generated Social‑Media Promo Packs"**
- **Problem:** Small businesses need fresh visual content but can’t afford a designer.
- **Solution:** Use a low‑cost AI image generator (e.g., Stable Diffusion via free tier) to produce a set of 10 unique promotional images for $30. Deliver in a zip with suggested captions.

**Idea 14 – “Custom AI‑Logo Sketches (Hand‑drawn Finish)"**
- **Problem:** Start‑ups want a distinctive logo but lack budget for a professional designer.
- **Solution:** Generate 3 AI concepts for $10, then hand‑refine one in Photoshop/Illustrator (your time). Price the final package at $120, keeping costs under $200 total.

---

## 7️⃣ Local Event Planning Help

**Idea 15 – “Pop‑Up “Photo‑Booth” Rental (DIY Kit)"**
- **Problem:** Small community events want a fun photo area but can’t afford a full‑service booth.
- **Solution:** Assemble a $180 kit: backdrop, ring‑light, tablet on a tripod running a free photo‑app, and printed instant‑print strips (via cheap bulk printer). Rent the kit for a day and provide on‑site setup.

---

### Quick‑Start Checklist (for you)
1. Choose 2‑3 ideas that match your skills/resources.  
2. Source the bulk items (printing, locks, kits) from local wholesalers or online discount sites.  
3. Set up a simple landing page (Google Sites, Carrd) and payment link (PayPal/Stripe).  
4. Market via local Facebook groups, campus boards, or flyers in the target area.

Each of these concepts can be launched with **≤ $200** and addresses a clear, under‑served problem in its niche.
