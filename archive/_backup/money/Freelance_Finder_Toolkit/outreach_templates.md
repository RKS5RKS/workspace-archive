# Client Outreach Templates

## 1. Cold Email Scripts

### Universal Cold Outreach
```
Write a cold email to [specific business type] introducing my services as a [niche] specialist.

Include:
- Personalized observation about their current website/marketing (make 2-3 specific observations)
- One relevant case study with measurable results
- Clear, low-friction CTA (not "book a call" - offer something free first)
- Signature with credentials

Tone: confident but not arrogant, helpful not salesy
Length: under 200 words
```

### Warm Outreach ( mutual connection)
```
Write a warm email to [prospect name] who was referred by [referrer name].

Include:
- Reference the mutual connection naturally
- Why I'm reaching out specifically to them
- Brief credential highlight
- Offer of value (not just "let's chat")
- CTA

Tone: warm, personal, not transactional
```

### Engagement-Based Outreach
```
Write a LinkedIn outreach message to someone who [engaged with my content/commented on my post/shares similar content].

Include:
- Reference their engagement specifically
- Bridge to my services naturally
- Social proof element
- Easy next step

Tone: genuine appreciation, natural follow-up
```

## 2. Follow-Up Sequences

### The "No Response" Follow-Up
```
Create a 3-email follow-up sequence for cold outreach with no response.

Email 1 (5 days after initial):
- Short, casual check-in
- Add new piece of value (tip, insight, case study)
- Reiterate offer

Email 2 (10 days after initial):
- Different angle - share a relevant mistake you noticed in their marketing
- Offer to help "no strings attached"
- Still low-friction CTA

Email 3 (21 days after initial):
- Break the pattern - longer, more personal
- Share a transformation story
- Ask one simple question
- Signal you're moving on (creates urgency)
```

### Post-Meeting Follow-Up
```
Create a follow-up sequence after a discovery call where prospect seemed interested but didn't book.

Email 1 (same day):
- Thank them for their time
- Recap what you discussed
- Attach relevant resources
- Clear next steps with calendar link

Email 2 (3 days later):
- Share something that came to mind after the call
- Address any objections they raised
- Add social proof relevant to their situation

Email 3 (7 days later):
- Check-in, low pressure
- Offer additional value
- Ask if they have questions
```

## 3. Portfolio Showcase

### Portfolio Introduction Email
```
Write a portfolio introduction email to send to warm prospects.

Include:
- Brief intro (1 sentence)
- 3 project highlights with specific results
- For each: challenge → solution → outcome
- Link to full portfolio
- CTA to discuss their specific needs

Format: scannable, results-focused
```

### Case Study Format
```
Create a case study template for [industry] clients.

For each case study provide:
- Client background (anonymized if needed)
- The problem they had
- The solution I provided
- Specific metrics/results (quantify everything)
- Client testimonial (or placeholder)
- Relevance to new prospect's situation

Make it adaptable to different project types
```

## 4. Proposal Templates

### Web Developer Proposal
```
Create a proposal template for a website redesign project.

Include:
- Executive summary
- Scope of work breakdown
- Timeline (phases)
- Investment options (3 tiers)
- What's included
- Not included (scope boundaries)
- Process overview
- Next steps

Make it comprehensive but scannable
```

### Ongoing Service Retainer Proposal
```
Create a proposal template for a monthly retainer arrangement.

Include:
- Value proposition (why ongoing beats one-time)
- Services included (tiered)
- Hours/month allocation
- Deliverables schedule
- Investment (monthly)
- Commitment period
- How to upgrade/downgrade
- Success metrics we'll track
```
