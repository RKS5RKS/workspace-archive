A self-proclaimed vigilante superhero known as 'NoMark' has been targeting a serial public urinator at the University of Minnesota campus in Minneapolis. Donning a homemade Batman-style costume, NoMark has taken matters into his own hands to stop the so-called 'campus pisser' through a series of confrontations and citizen's arrests, raising questions about the role of masked vigilantes in enhancing public safety.

## Why it matters

The case of NoMark and the 'campus pisser' highlights the growing trend of citizen-led crime fighting, as well as the complex ethical and legal issues surrounding vigilantism. While NoMark's actions may resonate with some frustrated residents, the use of masked identities and unauthorized policing tactics raises concerns about accountability and the rule of law.

## The details

According to reports, NoMark has been actively pursuing and confronting the 'campus pisser' on the University of Minnesota grounds for several months. In multiple incidents, NoMark has physically restrained the suspect and made citizen's arrests, only to see the suspect released shortly after by authorities. The 'campus pisser' is believed to be responsible for a string of public urination incidents that have plagued the campus, leading to increased frustration among students and staff.

- NoMark first appeared on the University of Minnesota campus in January 2026.
- The 'campus pisser' has been active for over a year, with the first reported incident occurring in February 2025.
